About me

Trained as a physicist with some experience as a telecom engineer, I'm now in the QERM program at UW. I found my way here in searching for a way to combine my quantitative training with my lifelong curiosity and appreciation for nature. I like using math to answer questions about sustainability and our relationships with natural resources.

I also love life in the city and find myself thinking about the parallels between what we do in natural resource management and how it can be applied to transportation and urban environments.

I'm active in my neighborhood improvement group, the Uptown Alliance, and was excited to help bring to life one of Seattle's first parklets. Some other things we're working on include pushing for a light rail station in Seattle Center as part of ST3 and helping activate Counterbalance Park.

As any numbers person will admit, I'm fascinated by data. Especially good data. If you check out my projects page, you'll find evidence of this.
