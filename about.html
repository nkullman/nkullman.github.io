About me

Trained as a physicist with some experience as a telecom engineer, now studying quantitative ecology and optimization at UW.

Work

I found QERM in searching for a way to apply my quantitative training to a meaningful problem motivated by conservation or sustainability.

Sándor Tóth introduced a number of such problems in "Optimization Techniques for Natural Resources". After seeing the power of integer programming and spatial optimization applied to these problems, I was hooked.

Under Sándor's supervision, I am now studying the impacts of climate change on optimal forest planning. I'm interested in learning how the changing climate's effects on forests may in turn affect our relationship with them as a source of biodiversity, natural resources, recreation, and beauty.

Take a look at my research page to learn more.

Not work

Outside my research in forestry, I enjoy life in the city. I'm active in my neighborhood improvement group, the Uptown Alliance, and was excited to help bring to life one of Seattle's first parklets. Some other things we're working on now include advocating for bike lanes and a light rail station in Seattle Center, improving Uptown's connection to South Lake Union, and helping invigorate Counterbalance Park.

I've dabbled a bit in using optimization in my daily life. One example: writing an optimization model to find flat running routes through the city. Spatial optimization plays a huge role in transportation and logistics, and mine is a pretty trivial example of how it can be done. If you want to see more, check out the INFORMS community for transportation science and logistics.

You can find info on the running routes model and other projects I've tinkered with here.
