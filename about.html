The Fonz had better speed up

Since first-year physics, calculating The Fonz's minimum launch speed to clear the record 14 garbage cans on his motorcycle, I've been fascinated by our ability to make predictions about nature through mathematics.

Quantitative ecology takes math into nature.

Interested in seeing mathematical rigor applied to aquatic and terrestrial ecosystems (and everywhere in between)? QERM is the place. Check out what other QERMies and QERM faculty are up to on their student and faculty webpages, or take a look at the QERMipedia.

About me

Trained as a physicist with some experience as a telecom engineer, now studying quantitative ecology at UW.

Where I am

Work

I found QERM in searching for a way to apply my quantitative training to a meaningful problem motivated by conservation or sustainability.

Sandor Toth introduced a number of such problems in "Optimization Techniques for Natural Resources". After seeing the power of integer programming and spatial optimization applied to these problems, I was hooked.

Under Sandor's supervision, I am now studying the impacts of climate change on forest planning. I'm interested in learning how the changing cliamte's effects on forests may in turn affect our relationship with them as a source of biodiversity, natural resources, recreation, and beauty.

Play

Outside my research in forestry, I enjoy life in the city. I'm active in my neighborhood improvement group, the Uptown Alliance, and was excited to help bring to life one of Seattle's first parklets. Some other things we're working on now include pushing for bike lanes and a light rail station in Seattle Center, improving Uptown's connection to South Lake Union, and helping invigorate Counterbalance Park.

I've dabbled a bit in combining work and play in writing an optimization model to find flat running routes through the city. It's a pretty trivial example of a much grander application: using spatial optimization in urban transportation.

You can find info on the running routes model and other projects I've tinkered with here.
