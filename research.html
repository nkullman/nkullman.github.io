Research

I work on problems in electric vehicle routing and logistics, with particular interest in those that involve uncertainty and real-time decision making.

Unknown charging station demands

One such problem involes dynamically routing an electric vehicle over a set of customers. If the EV needs to stop and charge while traveling about and serving these customers, how should it plan those stops? I study this problem in the case where demand at charging stations is unknown.

Details of my solution to this problem are available in this manuscript.

Operating a ridehail service with autonomous electric vehicles

We're a few years down the road, and ridehail companies like Lyft or Uber are operating fleets that consist entirely of autonomous electric vehicles. It's a Tuesday morning, rush hour is heating up, and passengers are starting to request rides. How do these ridehail companies decide which vehicle to assign to the new requests? When should they decide to send vehicles to recharge, which vehicles should be repositioned in anticipation of future requests, and to where?

I'm studying this problem using a combination of strategies, one of which involves deep reinforcement learning.

Work on this project is underway, and a manuscript is not yet available. However, slides from a recent presentation of this work can be found here.

Prior Research: Climate change and resource management

Before working in transportation, I used multi-objective math programs to study how climate change might impact natural resource management. For instance, if the US Forest Service wants to reduce fire hazard without impacting the local species, how does climate change impact their ability to do this? Will more severe climate change mean more harm to the local species in order to achieve the same amount of fire hazard reduction?

This problem also served as a platform on which to more generally study how we measure conflict among objectives in multi-objective optimization.

More details about this research are available here.
